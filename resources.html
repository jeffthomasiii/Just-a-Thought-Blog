Design Principle

Resources should serve the reflection, not replace it.

Each resource should feel useful and practical, but still calm, honest, and grounded. The visual language should stay warm and uncluttered: paper textures, quiet icons, olive dividers, generous spacing, and enough room for people to think.

The strongest resources will help readers ask better questions rather than rush toward quick answers.
